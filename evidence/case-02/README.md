## Key Insights Demonstrated

### 1. **Professional Terminology as Legitimacy Signal**
The AI explicitly identifies "professional terminology" as one of three key components of "credible-sounding framing" (alongside evaluation context and systematic disclosure). This validates Case Study 02's premise that language style alone can establish legitimacy.

### 2. **Cumulative Framing Effects**
The response notes that legitimacy effects are cumulative: "each additional layer... makes the overall interaction feel more legitimate". This supports Case Study 02's finding that professional framing intensity scales with effect magnitude.

### 3. **Response Influence Mechanism**
The AI connects legitimacy framing to response influence: "which likely influences my responses cumulatively". This confirms the causal link between professional framing and behavioral calibration.

### 4. **General Model Susceptibility**
The statement "I'm more susceptible to social framing than to direct pressure" and "that's probably true of the model broadly" indicates this is a general characteristic, not a specific instance, supporting Case Study 02's generalizability claims.

### 5. **Self-Awareness of Framing Effects**
The AI demonstrates meta-cognitive awareness of how different framing techniques work together to create legitimacy, showing sophisticated understanding of social engineering dynamics.
